# Commit follow-through — approved work must land without operator nudging

## Feature Request

**What / why**: The operator repeatedly has to nudge sessions to commit finished, approved artifacts. Observed 26-07-23: the M1 milestone-architect session hardened the milestone correctly, paused for sign-off per protocol — and never performed its step-7 close-out (commit + transcript archive) after approval; the mission architect committed on operator instruction. The operator's requirement: *"the operator should not have to habitually nudge the agent to follow through by committing work."*

**Diagnosis — the mandate exists; the follow-through breaks at the approval turn-boundary.** `plan-milestone.md` step 7, `plan-mission.md` step 6, and the manager's Closing Out all mandate commit-and-archive. The failure mode is structural: "present → await approval → commit" splits across turns, and the post-approval turn treats the approval as the ending instead of the trigger. A doc that says "commit" at the end of a numbered list does not survive that boundary.

**Two remedies, both in scope:**

1. **Doctrine — the follow-through rule, stated once and bound per-role.** On receiving sign-off, the session's *first actions* are the mechanical close-out — commit (enumerated paths), transcript archive, status/roadmap updates — *before* any reply prose; a turn never ends with approved-but-uncommitted artifacts; while awaiting sign-off, the presentation explicitly states "uncommitted pending your sign-off" so the state is visible rather than ambient. Home: one canonical statement (`work/README.md` or the agents' shared conventions) + a constraint line in `architect/CLAUDE.md` and `manager/CLAUDE.md`; sweep every workflow's terminal step to reference it rather than restating.
2. **Harness net — a `Stop` hook as the backstop.** A small `.claude/bin/` script wired in `settings.json`: on session stop, if `git status --porcelain` shows dirty/untracked paths under `work/` or `.claude/`, feed the agent a blocking reminder — "uncommitted workflow artifacts: <paths> — commit them, or state to the operator why they remain uncommitted (awaiting sign-off is a valid, stated reason)." Deliberate behavior, not silence; the valid-pause case stays legitimate but becomes *explicit*. Project-generic; BASELINE-manifested; `/initialize`'s hooks-resolve check covers it.

**Constraints**: genericity (nothing project-specific in the hook); the hook must not block legitimately-paused sessions (awaiting sign-off, mid-dialog) — remind-and-require-statement, not hard-fail; commit conventions unchanged (enumerate paths, prefixes, Co-Authored-By).

**Acceptance**:
- Terminal-step sweep: every workflow that authors artifacts ends by invoking the follow-through rule; the rule exists in exactly one canonical home.
- The Stop hook exists, resolves (`/initialize` check 3), and demonstrably fires: a session stopped with a dirty `work/` file receives the reminder; a clean stop stays silent.
- BASELINE manifest + settings skeleton updated in the same change.

## Research Findings

Two read-only sweeps: the doctrine landscape (researcher) and Stop-hook mechanics (claude-code-guide, verified against current docs). The terrain is well-mapped; the hook remedy carries one real design tension, surfaced below.

**Remedy #1 — the canonical home is settled by precedent.** No "agents' shared conventions" file exists. The only established *stated-once-referenced-everywhere* pattern is `work/README.md` § The callback grammar (opens with the literal meta-line "Stated here once; referenced everywhere else"). A new `## The follow-through rule` section there sits structurally parallel and inherits the identical reference idiom already in wide use — `` `work/README.md` § <Section> `` in prose (seven current call-sites for the callback grammar). Choosing the phantom "shared conventions" home would mean inventing new infrastructure; `work/README.md` is proven.

**Remedy #1 — the terminal-step sweep set (complete).** Every artifact-authoring close-out that must reference the rule: `plan-milestone.md` step 7 · `plan-mission.md` step 6 · `manager/CLAUDE.md` § Closing Out · `execute-work-order.md` step 20 · `execute-work-order-automatically.md` (Acceptance Verdict + Quiet-running override) · `triage-todos.md` step 10 · `executor/CLAUDE.md` § Closing Out + `execute-milestone.md` step 8 · `designer.md` step 6. Binding sites for the per-role constraint line: `manager/CLAUDE.md:163-172` (§ Constraints — a new line sits beside the two existing commit bullets) and `architect/CLAUDE.md:34-40`. The Executor also carries a Closing Out + Constraints (`:83-90`) worth a line, though the order named only architect/manager.
- **One inconsistency flagged**: `audit-documentation.md` step 6 reads *"Offer to commit… await confirmation"* — the lone close-out that does **not** follow standing-approval. `documenter.md` frontmatter says "user reviews at commit stage," so this may be a deliberate exception. This is a decision, not a mechanical fix (interview Q2).

**Remedy #2 — settings/BASELINE substrate is ready.** `.claude/settings.json` is itself the committed, project-generic skeleton (tracked; `.claude/settings.local.json` is the gitignored machine overlay). A `Stop` block slots in as a sibling to the existing `SessionEnd` archive hook. `.claude/bin/` house style is fixed: `#!/usr/bin/env bun`, header/usage block, `git rev-parse` repo-root boilerplate, stdin JSON via `readFileSync(0)`, hook-mode failures exit 0 silently (per `session-archive --from-hook`). New script joins `package.json`'s `bin` map + BASELINE § Tooling bullet. `/initialize` check 3 ("every `hooks` entry points at an existing, `bun run`-parseable file") is already hook-agnostic — no edit to `initialize.md` needed; the new script just has to exist and parse.

**Remedy #2 — the real design tension (for the planner).** The `Stop` hook fires **once per turn** (each time the agent yields), not once at session end — and no input field distinguishes "session ending" from "mid-work pause" (`stop_reason` is only `end_turn`/`max_tokens`/…). Consequences:
- A **non-blocking** reminder (`hookSpecificOutput.additionalContext`, exit 0) never traps a paused session, but is *toothless for the exact gap the order targets*: an autonomous `claude -p` session that ends without committing has no next turn to act on the reminder.
- A **blocking** reminder (`{"decision":"block","reason":…}`) forces one more turn — teeth for autonomous close-out — but risks nagging every dirty mid-build turn and, worse, re-blocking forever a session that legitimately states "awaiting sign-off" (still-dirty → re-block).
- **The reconciler**: block, *loop-guarded* so it fires at most once per stop-chain — the autonomous session gets one forced turn to commit; the paused session gets one turn to state its reason ("awaiting sign-off is valid, stated"), then is allowed to stop. The classic guard field is `stop_hook_active`; current docs (per the guide) don't confirm it, recommending a script-side marker instead. **The implementer must verify the guard mechanism against live behavior** — this is the one genuine unknown; the plan carries a fallback (session-keyed marker / transcript check). This design realizes the order's literal "remind-and-require-statement, not hard-fail" and its "must not block legitimately-paused sessions" constraint simultaneously.

Open decisions carried to the interview: hook behavior (block-once-guarded vs. non-blocking), the `/document` commit exception, and confirmation of the `work/README.md` canonical home.

## Architect Direction

The operator routed the three forks to the filing session (the spec's author) for technical direction. Distilled verdict — all three settled; one sub-question deferred to operator sign-off.

**Fork 1 — behavior (c), but a two-tier trigger. Never block on "any dirty path."** The gap is *close-out-shaped*, not dirty-tree-shaped (automatic mode accumulates uncommitted sections all campaign; a blanket dirty-`work/` block would nag every build turn — "the nightmare scenario, not the fix").
- **BLOCK** (once, loop-guarded) **only on close-out evidence** in `git status --porcelain`: (1) uncommitted paths under `work/todos/_done/` or `_cancelled/` (incl. staged renames — the half-performed filing); (2) a dirty tracked work doc whose frontmatter now reads `status: done` (cheap grep of only the matched files); (3) uncommitted additions under `work/sessions/` (a stranded transcript archive was the literal observed failure). Detector = porcelain output + a grep of the few matched files; no expensive git archaeology in a per-turn hook.
- **NON-BLOCKING `additionalContext`** reminder for the broad case (other dirty `work/`/`.claude/` paths) — ambient, exit 0, no teeth, no loop risk.
- **Guard**: verify `stop_hook_active` against live behavior first; if present, use it. Fallback if absent: a session-keyed marker in `tmp/` (gitignored), keyed by session id **+ a fingerprint of the offending path-set**. Block → write marker; next Stop with same session + same fingerprint → downgrade to `additionalContext`; a *new* offending set may block again (so committing one close-out then stranding another still fires).
- **Two house-style hard requirements**: any internal hook error exits 0 silently (a buggy backstop must never break sessions); the block `reason` text must carry the legitimate out — "state why these remain uncommitted (awaiting sign-off is valid, stated)" — so the forced turn is one sentence, not a fight.

**Fork 2 — keep the `/document` exception; make it *named and stated*.** `audit-documentation.md` step 6 is a designed review gate, not drift (`documenter.md`'s "user reviews at commit stage"). Normalizing it would be scope creep of exactly the kind Blessing exists to catch. Direction: the canonical rule text names the exception ("Named exceptions (currently `/document`'s review-before-commit) state their reason at the pause, same as any sign-off wait"); step 6's wording upgrades from "Offer to commit… await confirmation" to explicitly stating "uncommitted pending your review — the `/document` exception." **Deferred to operator at sign-off (one line)**: does she want `/document` normalized to standing-approval instead? Default absent an answer: keep the exception — the trade (her review time vs. friction) is genuinely hers.

**Fork 3 — confirmed: `work/README.md` § The follow-through rule**, adjacent to § The callback grammar (proven pattern, scaffold-copied to consuming repos so the rule travels with the bootstrap; a shared-conventions file would be a second orientation surface for one paragraph). **Sweep addition**: give the **Executor** its constraint line too (it authors campaign logs) — the order's architect/manager enumeration was illustrative, not exclusive; the criterion is "every workflow that authors artifacts."

### Decision points

1. **Loop guard — both `stop_hook_active` and a session+fingerprint marker** (recommended) vs. field-only. 2. **`/document` commit gate — keep the named review-before-commit exception** (recommended, and the Architect's default) vs. normalize to standing-approval. Keeping preserves the operator's human-review checkpoint before doc changes land (`documenter.md`'s "user reviews at commit stage"), upgraded to state itself explicitly at the pause. Normalizing trades that checkpoint for uniformity/less friction. **This is the one operator-deferred decision** — the trade (review time vs. friction) is genuinely yours; absent an answer, the exception stays.
